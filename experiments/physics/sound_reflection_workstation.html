Sound Reflection Workstation Experiment

Study echo, sound reflection, and the effect of distance and surface materials.

Theory

Study echo, sound reflection, and the effect of distance and surface materials. To study the reflection of sound, determine the conditions for hearing an echo, and explore how surface materials affect sound absorption.

Formula

Principle: To study the reflection of sound, determine the conditions for hearing an echo, and explore how surface materials affect sound absorption.

Aim

To study the reflection of sound, determine the conditions for hearing an echo, and explore how surface materials affect sound absorption.

Apparatus

  • Two cardboard tubes
  • Smooth reflecting surface (wall)
  • Stopwatch
  • Measuring tape
  • Sound source (clap / buzzer)

Procedure

  1. Stand at a known distance from a large reflecting surface (wall).
  2. Produce a sharp sound (clap) and listen for the echo.
  3. Measure the time between the original sound and the echo.
  4. Calculate speed of sound: v = 2d/t.
  5. Repeat at different distances.
  6. Test different reflecting surfaces (concrete, wood, glass, curtain).
  7. Observe: echo is heard only when t ≥ 0.1 s (minimum distance ≈ 17 m).
