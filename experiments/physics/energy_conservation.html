Work, Energy & Conservation Experiment

Roller-coaster simulation demonstrating PE ↔ KE conversion and conservation of energy.

Theory

Roller-coaster simulation demonstrating PE ↔ KE conversion and conservation of energy. To demonstrate the conservation of mechanical energy and the interconversion of kinetic and potential energy using a roller-coaster track simulation.

Formula

Principle: To demonstrate the conservation of mechanical energy and the interconversion of kinetic and potential energy using a roller-coaster track simulation.

Aim

To demonstrate the conservation of mechanical energy and the interconversion of kinetic and potential energy using a roller-coaster track simulation.

Apparatus

  • Roller-coaster track (virtual)
  • Ball of variable mass
  • Height-adjustable ramp
  • Energy meter display

Procedure

  1. Set the initial height and mass of the ball.
  2. Release the ball from rest and observe it gaining speed as it descends (PE → KE).
  3. Observe the ball slowing as it climbs (KE → PE).
  4. Monitor the energy bars and graph to verify total energy stays constant.
  5. Enable friction and observe energy loss as heat.
  6. Try different track shapes by dragging control points.
