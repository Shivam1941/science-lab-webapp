Motion Simulator Experiment

Visualize position, velocity, and acceleration graphs for uniform and accelerated motion.

Theory

Visualize position, velocity, and acceleration graphs for uniform and accelerated motion. To study different types of motion (uniform, uniformly accelerated, decelerated) and understand the relationships between displacement, velocity, and acceleration.

Formula

Principle: To study different types of motion (uniform, uniformly accelerated, decelerated) and understand the relationships between displacement, velocity, and acceleration.

Aim

To study different types of motion (uniform, uniformly accelerated, decelerated) and understand the relationships between displacement, velocity, and acceleration.

Apparatus

  • Ticker tape timer (or motion sensor)
  • Trolley / Cart on track
  • Power supply for ticker timer
  • Ruler / Measuring tape
  • Inclined plane (for acceleration)

Procedure

  1. Set up the ticker timer and thread the tape.
  2. Give the trolley a push for uniform motion — observe equal spacing.
  3. Use inclined plane for accelerated motion — observe increasing spacing.
  4. Calculate velocity for each interval using v = Δs/Δt.
  5. Plot position-time, velocity-time, and acceleration-time graphs.
