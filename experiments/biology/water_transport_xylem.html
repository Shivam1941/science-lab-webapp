Water Transport in Plants (Xylem) Experiment

Demonstrate how water travels through xylem vessels using a coloured dye and celery / white flower stem.

Theory

Demonstrate how water travels through xylem vessels using a coloured dye and celery / white flower stem. To demonstrate that water and dissolved substances are transported upward through xylem vessels in plants using a coloured dye experiment.

Formula

Principle: To demonstrate that water and dissolved substances are transported upward through xylem vessels in plants using a coloured dye experiment.

Aim

To demonstrate that water and dissolved substances are transported upward through xylem vessels in plants using a coloured dye experiment.

Apparatus

  • Fresh celery stalk with leaves (or white carnation)
  • Glass beaker / jar (250 mL)
  • Food colouring / coloured dye (red, blue, or green)
  • Sharp knife / blade
  • Water
  • Ruler
  • Clock / stopwatch

Procedure

  1. Select a fresh celery stalk or white flower stem and make a fresh diagonal cut at the base.
  2. Fill the beaker with water and add several drops of food colouring. Stir well.
  3. Place the freshly cut stem into the coloured water immediately.
  4. Leave the setup in a bright, warm location for 2–4 hours (or overnight for deeper colour).
  5. Observe gradual colour change in the stem, leaves, and petals.
  6. Optionally, cut the stem cross-sectionally to observe coloured xylem vessels.
