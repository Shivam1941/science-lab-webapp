Photosynthesis – Starch Test (Iodine)

Theory

To test for the presence of starch in a leaf and prove that light and chlorophyll are necessary for photosynthesis using the iodine test.

Procedure

  • Destarch the plant by keeping it in a dark cupboard for 48 hours.
  • Cover part of a leaf with black paper secured with paper clips.
  • Place the plant in sunlight for 4–6 hours.
  • Pluck the leaf and boil it in water for 5 minutes to kill cells and stop reactions.
  • Transfer the leaf to a beaker of alcohol and heat in a water bath until the leaf turns pale/white (chlorophyll removed).
  • Remove the leaf, rinse with water, and spread it flat in a petri dish.
  • Add iodine solution drops over the entire leaf.
  • Observe: the exposed area turns blue-black (starch present); the covered area stays brown/yellow (no starch).

Apparatus

  • Potted plant (destarched for 48 hrs in dark)
  • Black paper and clips
  • Beaker with water
  • Beaker with alcohol (ethanol)
  • Bunsen burner / hot plate
  • Iodine solution
  • Petri dish
  • Forceps
  • Water bath
